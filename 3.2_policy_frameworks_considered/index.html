Three Key Frameworks in Policy Change

Advocacy Coalition Framework (ACF)

  • Coalitions formed around shared beliefs and interests
  • Compete to shape policy outcomes
  • Policy change driven by learning and external events

Punctuated Equilibrium Theory (PET)

  • Long periods of stability punctuated by sudden, intense changes
  • Policy image and venue shopping play key roles
  • External shocks like crises can shift the equilibrium

Multiple Streams Approach

  • Problem, Policy, and Politics streams
  • Policy windows: critical opportunities for change
  • Policy entrepreneurs influence which issues rise on the agenda

Real-World Example: Climate change policy discussions following natural disasters.

Incrementalism in Policy Making

Incrementalism and Bounded Rationality

  • Explains stability and gradual changes in the policy process
  • Actors operate under bounded rationality
  • Policy changes occur through successive limited comparisons

Activity: Map out a recent local policy change that followed an incremental process. We will discuss this during the whiteboard session.

Critique of Incrementalism

  • Cannot fully explain radical policy shifts
  • Assumes that actors prefer small, safe changes
  • May miss opportunities for significant reforms

Complexity of the Policy Process

  • Multiple actors and institutions interact, creating complexity
  • Identifying causal relationships can be difficult
  • Using multiple frameworks helps capture this complexity

Discussion: How do multiple frameworks enhance our understanding of complex policy environments?

Power and Agenda Setting

  • Who has the power to set the agenda?
  • Which actors influence what gets on the agenda?
  • How does power shift over time?

Critical Review of Frameworks

  • Each framework offers insights but also has limitations
  • Multiple perspectives are necessary to understand the policy process
  • None of the frameworks alone provide a complete picture

Takeaway: Policymaking is a dynamic, multifaceted process influenced by numerous factors.
